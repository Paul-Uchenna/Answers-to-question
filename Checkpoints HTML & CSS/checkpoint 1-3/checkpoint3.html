African So Good Eats

Appetizers

  • Bruschetta and Crostini

    Toasted bread topped with various ingredients like tomatoes, basil, olive oil, and cheese.

  • Charcuterie and Cheese Boards

    Assortments of cured meats, cheeses, fruits, nuts, and crackers.

  • Caprese Salad

    Slices of fresh mozzarella, tomatoes, and basil, drizzled with balsamic glaze or olive oil.

  • Deviled Eggs:

    Hard-boiled eggs halved and filled with a mixture of egg yolks, mayonnaise, mustard, and spices.

Main Courses

  • Thieboudienne

    Often considered the national dish of Senegal, Thieboudienne is a flavorful rice and fish stew made with tomatoes, onions, carrots, cabbage, and various spices. The fish is typically stuffed with a mixture of parsley, garlic, and lemon before being cooked with the rice.

  • Yassa Poulet

    A tangy and aromatic dish featuring marinated chicken (or sometimes fish) cooked in a sauce made from onions, lemon juice, mustard, and vinegar. It's often served with rice and can be quite spicy.

  • Mafe

    Also known as groundnut stew, Mafe is a hearty dish made with meat (usually lamb or beef), vegetables, and a peanut-based sauce. It's seasoned with garlic, ginger, and other spices and often served with rice or couscous.

  • Domoda

    A thick and hearty stew made with meat (usually beef or chicken), vegetables, and ground peanuts. It's seasoned with garlic, onions, and tomato paste, and served with rice or couscous.

Dessert

  • Chin Chin

    Chin Chin is a crunchy, fried snack made from a dough of flour, sugar, milk, and sometimes eggs. It's flavored with nutmeg or vanilla and cut into small squares or strips before frying until golden brown.

  • Akara

    Akara, or Bean Cakes, are deep-fried fritters made from peeled black-eyed peas or beans blended with onions, peppers, and spices. While often enjoyed as a savory breakfast or snack, Akara can also be made sweet by adding sugar or sweeteners to the batter.

  • Fruit Salad

    A refreshing and healthy dessert option, Fruit Salad is made by combining a variety of fresh fruits such as watermelon, pineapple, apples, bananas, oranges, and grapes. It's often served chilled and may be topped with yogurt or honey for added sweetness.

  • Moi Moi

    While primarily a savory dish, Moi Moi, or Steamed Bean Pudding, can be sweetened and enjoyed as a dessert. The traditional recipe includes blended beans, onions, peppers, and spices, but sweet variations may incorporate sugar, coconut milk, or fruit puree for added sweetness.
